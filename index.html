Writs & Decrees makes small, careful tools. Our apps are built around one rule: whatever they measure about you stays on the device that measured it. Nothing is uploaded, because there's nowhere for it to go.

Our approach

How data moves through our apps

01

A reading is taken

The phone's own sensor, or a paired device, produces a value the same way any app would.

02

It's graphed on the spot

The reading is plotted straight into a local chart. The work happens on the chip already in your hand.

03

It stays where it landed

The value is written to storage on the device and nowhere else. We don't operate a server for it to reach.

Data principles

What we won't build

01

No accounts

There's nothing to sign up for. An account needs a server to check it against, and we don't run one for our health apps.

02

No analytics

Our apps don't report usage, crashes, or events back to us. We hear about problems the way a friend would tell us — directly.

03

No cloud backup

Health history lives in local storage. Back up your phone the way you already do, and it travels with the rest of your data — not to us.

04

No hidden export

If data ever leaves one of our apps, it's because you generated and shared it yourself. We don't build a second, quieter channel.

About Writs & Decrees

Writs & Decrees is a small company building privacy-focused software. We start from the assumption that data is personal, not a product opportunity, and we design every app around keeping it that way.

We keep our apps narrow on purpose. Each one is built to do a single thing well, store what it needs on the device it runs on, and stop there.
